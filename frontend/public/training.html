Quot PSE — Training Guide

This guide walks you through every task you'll perform on Quot PSE, from signing in on day one to filing your first IPSAS monthly pack. It is structured by module, and within each module by the specific workflow you're likely to run.

💡 Tip: You don't need to read cover-to-cover. Use the sidebar to jump straight to the workflow you need — each walkthrough is self-contained with prerequisites listed up front.

How to read each walkthrough

Every walkthrough follows the same shape so you always know where to look:

  1. Purpose — what the task achieves in business terms.e.g. "Release a warrant so downstream MDAs can draw cash."
  2. Prerequisites — what must be true before you start.Roles, data that needs to exist, period status.
  3. Steps — numbered actions in the UI.Exactly what to click, what to type, what to expect.
  4. What you'll see — a mockup of the screen.So you can confirm you're on the right page.
  5. Tips & warnings — things that trip up new users.Green = helpful; amber = be careful; red = will block you.

Getting started — First login

Your tenant administrator will create your account and email you a temporary password. Your first task is to sign in, change that password, and confirm you can see the dashboard.

Sign in to your tenant

  1. Open your browser and go to https://your-state.quotpse.ng.Your State-specific URL was given to you at provisioning. For Delta State it would be delta.quotpse.ng.
  2. Enter the username or email you were given.
  3. Enter the temporary password from your welcome email.
  4. Click Sign In. You'll land on the dashboard.
  5. Open the My Account menu (bottom-left of sidebar) → Change Password.
  6. Set a new password of at least 12 characters with a mix of letters, numbers and a symbol.
Login screen — the brand lockup appears on the left, the sign-in form on the right.
⚠ If login fails: check you're on the correct tenant URL (each State has its own subdomain). "Wrong password" after 5 tries will temporarily lock your account — wait 10 minutes or contact your admin.

The dashboard & sidebar — your home base

After login you land on the Government Financial Dashboard. It gives you four headline numbers at the top and visual summaries below.

The dashboard — KPIs on top, visual summaries below, Quick Actions row at the bottom.

Anatomy of every screen

  1. Sidebar (left) — module navigation. Click a module name to expand sub-items, or click again to navigate to its main page.
  2. Breadcrumb + page header (top) — shows where you are. The Back button always returns to the previous list page.
  3. Content area (center) — the table, form or report you're working on.
  4. Notification bell (top-right on some pages) — shows unread alerts. Click to see recent items.
  5. My Account / Sign Out (bottom-left) — profile, password change, sign out.

Common UI patterns

These patterns appear on almost every module — once you learn them here, you know them everywhere.

List + detail drawer New + form Status pill Approval chain Export menu

1. List + detail drawer

Most modules open to a table of rows (Appropriations, Journals, POs…). Click a row to open a side drawer with full details, related actions (Approve, Post, Reverse), and an audit history.

2. New + form

The New ____ button (top-right on every list page) opens a form. Required fields are marked with *. Forms save as Draft so you can come back later.

3. Status pills

A coloured pill shows the current state of every document:

DRAFT PENDING APPROVED / POSTED / ACTIVE REJECTED / CLOSED / VOID

4. Export menu

Every report page has an Export button with four formats: JSON HTML XLSX PDF.

💡 Tip — universal keyboard shortcuts: Esc closes drawers · / focuses search · Enter submits forms · Ctrl/Cmd + K opens the global search (where available).
Module 01

Budget & Appropriation

The legal gateway for every naira spent. You'll use it to create Appropriations for the fiscal year, route them through the House of Assembly workflow, release monthly warrants, and monitor execution.

Who uses it: Budget & Appropriation Manager Budget Officer Accountant-General (sign-off)

Walkthrough 1 — Create a new Appropriation

Purpose: record an appropriation line from the Appropriation Act so the downstream MDAs can draw against it.

Prerequisites:

  • A Fiscal Year must be open (Settings → Fiscal Year).
  • NCoA segments must be seeded (Administrative / Economic / Fund at minimum).
  • You have the Budget Officer or Budget Manager role.
  1. Open Budget & Appropriation → Appropriations from the sidebar.
  2. Click New Appropriation (top-right).
  3. In the Budget Header panel, pick the Fiscal Year, Administrative (MDA) and Fund Source.Administrative is searchable — start typing the MDA name, e.g. "Educ…".
  4. Leave Appropriation Type as Original Appropriation for the first pass. Set Law Reference (e.g. Appropriation Act 2026) and Enactment Date.
  5. Scroll to Budget Lines. On the first line pick an Economic Code (e.g. 22010100 — Salary), then Function (COFOG) and Programme if required.
  6. Type the Amount (NGN) and a short description.
  7. Click Add Line to enter more lines as needed. The total at the bottom must equal the approved figure on the Appropriation Act.
  8. Click Save Line(s). The status will be DRAFT.
  9. Open the saved row and click Submit to Legislature to advance it to SUBMITTED status. From there, your approver chain runs through APPROVED → ENACTED → ACTIVE.

⚠ Hard stop — economic code class: the Budget Lines table enforces classification rules. Expenditure codes (2x) and Asset codes (3x) are hard stops — you cannot over-commit. Revenue codes (1x) are statistical targets only; actuals can exceed them.

Walkthrough 2 — Release a Warrant / AIE

Purpose: issue the monthly drawing authority against an Active appropriation.

  1. Open Budget & Appropriation → Warrants / AIE.
  2. Click New Warrant.
  3. Pick the parent Appropriation row. The system will show the ceiling and amount already released to date.
  4. Enter Amount Requested and Warrant Date.
  5. Attach supporting documents (PDF scan of the Governor's warrant) — drag into the upload zone.
  6. Click Submit for Approval.
  7. Once approved up to the Accountant-General level, the warrant flips to RELEASED. Downstream Procurement and Treasury can now draw.

Walkthrough 3 — Run the Budget Execution Report

  1. Open Budget & Appropriation → Execution Report.
  2. Pick the Fiscal Year at the top.
  3. (Optional) Filter by Administrative, Fund, or Economic segment.
  4. The report shows, per row: Approved, Committed, Expended, Available, and Execution %.
  5. Click the Export button to download XLSX or PDF for distribution.
💡 Tip: the committed/expended numbers refresh instantly after every PO approval or vendor invoice — no overnight batch.

Common issues

Budget-exceeded error when approving a PO
The appropriation has insufficient available balance. Options: (a) raise a Supplementary Appropriation, (b) do a Virement from another appropriation with surplus, or (c) reduce the PO amount.
Totals on the dashboard don't match the Execution Report
Your cached totals are stale. A Tenant Admin should run resync_appropriation_totals from the command palette, or wait for the next transaction — totals refresh automatically on any commit / close event.
Module 02

General Ledger & Accounting (IPSAS)

The double-entry ledger at the heart of Quot PSE. Every transaction from every other module ends up here as a balanced journal.

Who uses it: Accountant-General Account Officer

Walkthrough 1 — Raise a manual journal

Purpose: record a correction, reclassification or non-system event in the GL.

Prerequisites: the fiscal period for posting_date must be OPEN.

  1. Open General Ledger from the sidebar.
  2. Click New Journal.
  3. Fill the header: Posting Date, MDA, Fund, Description, Reference Number (optional).
  4. In the Lines grid, add at least two rows:One debit line, one credit line. Each line must have either a debit amount OR a credit amount — never both, never zero.
  5. Pick the Account (NCoA Economic segment) and the full NCoA Code for each line.
  6. (Optional) Set a Cost Center (MDA) if the line should be allocated to a specific department.
  7. Verify the footer shows Debits = Credits. Save.
  8. The journal is in Draft status. Open it and click Route for Approval.
🛑 DB-level guarantee: the database rejects any line where both debit and credit are set, or where both are zero. You physically cannot save an unbalanced journal.

Walkthrough 2 — Post a journal to the GL

Who: Accountant-General (or delegated posting approver). Purpose: make the journal real in the GL balances.

  1. Open the Approval Inbox (sidebar → Approvals → Inbox).
  2. Locate the Journal row. Click to open.
  3. Review the lines, the period status, and the total. The system will have pre-run the balance check.
  4. Click Post Journal.
  5. You'll see a confirmation dialog listing the period, total debit and total credit. Click Confirm Post.
  6. On success, the journal status flips to Posted, GL balances update, and cached IPSAS reports for this fiscal year are invalidated so the next dashboard refresh is fresh.

Walkthrough 3 — Post a direct vendor invoice (no PO)

For utilities, rent, recurring services where there's no purchase order upstream.

  1. Go to General Ledger → Accounts Payable → New Invoice.
  2. Pick the Vendor, enter Invoice Number and Invoice Date.
  3. In the Lines grid, pick the expense Account (e.g. 22020100 — Utility services), enter the amount, and optionally a WHT rate (5% for contracts, 10% for consultancy).
  4. Save as Draft, then click Post Invoice. The service creates a Dr Expense / Cr AP journal and routes it for approval.
  5. Once posted, the expense lands in the GL and the Appropriation's expended total updates.

Walkthrough 4 — Run bank reconciliation

  1. Open General Ledger → Bank & Cash.
  2. Pick the bank account to reconcile.
  3. Click New Reconciliation Run.
  4. Upload the bank statement CSV or enter the closing balance as per the statement and the as-of-date.
  5. The screen shows two columns: GL lines vs Bank lines. Drag to match, or use Auto-match for exact amount+date pairs.
  6. Anything unmatched becomes a reconciling item. Resolve each one with a comment.
  7. Click Complete Reconciliation. The run is archived and all matched lines are marked reconciled.
💡 Tip: run reconciliation end-of-day on clearing accounts and at least weekly on operating accounts. Period close blocks if any account has an unreconciled statement older than 30 days.
Module 03

Treasury & Banking (TSA)

Consolidates cash across every bank account into a single TSA view. Tag FAAC inflows, track clearing, and run cash-flow forecasts against the warrant schedule.

Walkthrough 1 — View the TSA cash position

  1. Open Treasury & Banking (TSA).
  2. The top cards group cash by category: Main TSA, Revenue, Holding, Sub-Account, Consolidated.
  3. Click any card to expand into the individual account list.
  4. For each account you see: Account name · GL code · Currency · Current balance · Last reconciliation date.

Walkthrough 2 — Record a FAAC allocation receipt

  1. Open General Ledger → Accounts Receivable → Incoming Payments.
  2. Click New Receipt.
  3. Set the Receipt Type to FAAC Statutory (or FAAC VAT / Derivation / Excess Crude as appropriate).
  4. Pick the Bank Account that received the funds.
  5. Enter the Amount and the Value Date from the bank advice.
  6. Save. The receipt auto-posts a Dr Bank / Cr FAAC Revenue journal, and the TSA cash card reflects the change instantly.

Walkthrough 3 — Register a new bank account

  1. Open Settings → Bank Accounts.
  2. Click Add Account.
  3. Set Type (Bank / Cash / Petty Cash / Imprest).
  4. Pick the matching GL Account (typically under 10100xxx — Cash and Bank).
  5. Enter Account Number, Bank Name, Currency, TSA Category, and Opening Balance.
  6. Save. The account now appears on the Treasury dashboard.
Module 04

NCoA Classification

The 6-segment National Chart of Accounts that every journal line, appropriation and budget entry is coded against.

Understanding the 6 segments

SegmentWidthExampleMeaning
Administrative801100100Which MDA (Ministry, Dept, Agency) owns the transaction
Economic822010100Revenue / Expenditure / Asset class (like an IFRS account)
Functional870110000COFOG service area (Education, Health, Defence…)
Programme1401010000000000Policy programme (e.g. Universal Basic Education)
Fund501100Source of funds (CRF / Capital / FAAC / Donor…)
Geographic9050100010Zone · State · LGA (optional)

Walkthrough — Browse & add a new segment entry

  1. Open NCoA Classification → Economic (or any segment) from the sidebar.
  2. The table shows the hierarchy as a tree — click ▸ to expand.
  3. To add a new child: select the parent row and click New.
  4. Enter Code, Name, and Description.
  5. Save. The new code immediately becomes available in journal entry, appropriation and budget dropdowns.
ℹ Composite code: when a journal line is saved, Quot PSE concatenates all six segments into a 52-digit string stored as ncoa_code. This is what the OAGF Treasury returns file uses.
Module 05

Procurement

The full purchase cycle — from requisition to 3-way matched invoice — with commitment accounting that locks appropriation balance at PO approval.

Walkthrough 1 — Register a vendor

  1. Open Procurement → Vendors.
  2. Click Add New Vendor.
  3. Enter Vendor Name, TIN (Tax ID), RC number, address, bank details.
  4. Pick a Vendor Category (Contractor, Consultant, Supplier, Service Provider…).
  5. Save. The vendor is in PENDING status until the registration fee is confirmed paid.
  6. Go to Registration Invoice, generate an invoice, and record the payment. The vendor flips to ACTIVE.

Walkthrough 2 — Create a Purchase Requisition (PR)

  1. Open Procurement → Purchase Requisitions → New Requisition.
  2. Enter Description, Priority, required Delivery Date.
  3. In the Items grid: pick an Item (or enter free text), set Quantity, Unit Price, and Unit of Measure.
  4. Pick the Appropriation this PR will draw from. The system shows the available balance.
  5. Attach any supporting documents (specifications, quotes).
  6. Click Submit for Approval. Status becomes PENDING and routes to your approver.

Walkthrough 3 — Convert approved PR to a Purchase Order

  1. From the approved PR detail page, click Convert to PO.
  2. The PO form pre-fills vendor, lines, appropriation and amounts from the PR. Adjust if needed.
  3. Pick the Delivery Location (warehouse / MDA).
  4. Save as Draft, review, then click Submit for Approval.
  5. When the Procurement Manager approves, the PO flips to APPROVED and a Commitment is created against the Appropriation — the appropriation's available balance drops by the PO amount.

Walkthrough 4 — Post a Goods Received Note (GRN)

  1. When goods arrive, go to Procurement → GRN → New GRN.
  2. Pick the source PO. The lines pre-fill with quantities outstanding.
  3. Enter Quantity Received per line (may be less than PO quantity for partial deliveries).
  4. Pick the receiving Warehouse, and optionally Batch/Serial data for trackable items.
  5. Save, then Post GRN. Stock is updated and the commitment flips from ACTIVE to INVOICED.

Walkthrough 5 — Three-way matching (invoice verification)

  1. When the vendor invoice arrives, go to Procurement → Invoice Verification → New Verification.
  2. Pick the PO. The system fetches all GRNs and computes the matched quantity / value.
  3. Enter the Vendor Invoice Number, Invoice Date, and Total Amount.
  4. The 3-way check runs automatically:✔ Quantity (Invoice ≤ GRN) · ✔ Price (Invoice unit price ≤ PO unit price) · ✔ Vendor match · ✔ NCoA code match.
  5. If all four pass → status is MATCHED. Click Post to GL.
  6. If any fail → status is VARIANCE. Add a comment explaining the variance and route for AG override.

Module 06

HR & Nigerian Payroll

Employee directory, leave, attendance and a payroll engine that computes PAYE, Pension, NHF, NSITF and ITF per statute.

Walkthrough 1 — Add a new employee

  1. Open Human Resources → Employee Directory.
  2. Click New Employee.
  3. Enter personal details: Full Name, DOB, Gender, NIN, Phone, Email.
  4. Pick the Department, Position, Grade Level, and Step.
  5. Set Date of First Appointment, Date of Current Appointment.
  6. On the Payroll tab: enter Basic Salary, assign to Pension Scheme (PFA), enter PFA Code.
  7. On the Bank tab: account name, number, sort code.
  8. Save. The employee is ACTIVE and will be included in the next payroll run.

Walkthrough 2 — Run monthly payroll

  1. Open Human Resources → Payroll.
  2. Click New Payroll Run.
  3. Pick the Month and Year. Confirm the Fiscal Year.
  4. Pick the employee scope (All · By Department · By Grade Level).
  5. Click Calculate. The engine computes, per employee: Basic, Allowances, Gross, then deducts PAYE (progressive bands), Pension (8%), NHF (2.5%), Union dues, Loan instalments → Net.
  6. Review the preview. Click Approve & Post to create the GL journal (Dr Salary expense / Cr Net payable / Cr Statutory payables).
  7. Trigger Disbursement to queue the bank upload file (.csv) for your bank's bulk-payment portal.
💡 Tip: the PAYE computation uses the current year's PIT Act bands. Your tenant admin can review the bands under Settings → Tax before each run.

Walkthrough 3 — Social benefit batch pay (pensioners, widows)

  1. Open Human Resources → Social Benefits.
  2. Pick the benefit scheme (e.g. Pensioners under the Pension Act).
  3. Click New Batch. Pick the pay period.
  4. The beneficiaries list loads with amount per head. Review and edit individual amounts if required.
  5. Click Approve & Post. A single aggregated journal posts to the GL, and a bank-upload file is generated.
Module 07

Government Stores

Per-MDA store ledger. GRN receipts flow in here automatically; issues to departments hit the store ledger and the expense GL in a single transaction.

Walkthrough 1 — Add a new item

  1. Open Government Stores → Items → New Item.
  2. Enter Name, SKU, Description.
  3. Pick Product Type (Good / Service / Asset), Category, Unit of Measure.
  4. Pick the Valuation Method (FIFO / Weighted Average).
  5. Pick the Inventory GL Account and Expense GL Account.
  6. Set Reorder Point and Reorder Quantity if you want low-stock alerts.
  7. Save.

Walkthrough 2 — Issue stock to a department

  1. Open Government Stores → Issues → New Issue.
  2. Pick the issuing Warehouse and the receiving MDA / Department.
  3. Add item lines with quantity.
  4. Click Post Issue. Stock drops; a Dr Expense / Cr Inventory journal posts automatically.
Module 08

Workflow & Approvals

One approval matrix drives every document. Your Approval Inbox shows everything that needs your action across all modules.

Walkthrough 1 — Act on items in your Approval Inbox

  1. Open Workflow → Approval Inbox.
  2. Filter by Document Type (Journal, PO, Warrant, PV, Invoice…) or Urgency if needed.
  3. Click a row to open the detail drawer with full context (lines, attachments, approval chain so far).
  4. Click Approve → add an optional comment → confirm.
  5. Or click Reject → comment is required → the document goes back to the initiator.
  6. The item disappears from your inbox and appears in the next approver's inbox (or is posted if you were the final approver).

Walkthrough 2 — Set up out-of-office delegation

  1. Open Workflow → Delegations → New Delegation.
  2. Pick the Delegate user.
  3. Set the Start Date and End Date.
  4. (Optional) Scope the delegation to specific document types.
  5. Save. While active, all your pending approvals will also appear in the delegate's inbox.

Walkthrough 3 — Request a dual-control override

When: exceptional cases like a genuinely back-dated journal, a post-period adjustment, or a PO approval beyond the appropriation ceiling that has legitimate supplementary-in-flight justification.

  1. On the document drawer, click Request Override.
  2. Enter the Reason (required — shown permanently in the audit log).
  3. Pick the Second Approver — must be a different AG-level user.
  4. Submit. The second AG receives an urgent notification.
  5. When the second AG approves, the override is recorded, the document posts, and both signatures are stamped on the Override Audit page.
🛑 Overrides are permanent: every override is in the immutable audit log. Don't use them routinely — auditors look at the frequency of overrides as a control-environment indicator.
Module 09

Statutory Reporting

XSD-validated XML exports for FIRS (WHT, VAT) and PENCOM (pension schedule). What you download is what the regulator portal accepts.

Walkthrough — File monthly FIRS WHT

  1. Open General Ledger → Statutory → FIRS WHT.
  2. Pick the Period Year and Period Month.
  3. Click Generate Schedule. The system aggregates every WHT-bearing invoice / PV posted in the period into line items.
  4. Review the schedule. Each row shows: Payee Name · TIN · Invoice # · Gross · WHT Rate · WHT Amount · Nature of Payment.
  5. Pick Return Type (Original / Amended) and enter Prepared By.
  6. Click Export XML. The file downloads as FIRS-WHT-<state>-<YYYYMM>.xml.
  7. The file is XSD-validated before download. If anything fails validation you'll see a blocking error with the offending row.
  8. Upload the XML to the FIRS TaxPro portal. Record the acknowledgement number back in Quot PSE under Filings.

The flow for VAT and PENCOM follows the same 8-step pattern with schema-specific fields.

Module 10

IPSAS Financial Statements

The monthly management pack. Every report is live from the GL with prior-year comparatives and export buttons.

The five primary statements

  1. Statement of Financial Position (SoFP) — IPSAS 1 balance sheet with Current and Non-Current Assets / Liabilities.
  2. Statement of Financial Performance (SoFPerf) — IPSAS 1 income & expense.
  3. Cash Flow Statement — IPSAS 2, direct method.
  4. Statement of Changes in Net Assets — opening net assets + surplus/deficit + transfers → closing.
  5. Notes to the Financial Statements — minimum disclosure set with links to the GL rows that feed each note.

Walkthrough — Generate and export the monthly management pack

  1. Open IPSAS Reporting → Statement of Financial Position.
  2. Pick the Fiscal Year and the Period (month). Leave Comparative ticked to show prior-year figures.
  3. The report loads from the Redis cache if it's been generated in the last 10 minutes, otherwise it computes live (1-3 seconds on typical tenants).
  4. Click Export → XLSX to download for the AG's signature pack.
  5. Repeat for the remaining four statements. Tick Persist Snapshot on each so auditors can retrieve the exact filed version later.
ℹ Cache behaviour: reports auto-invalidate when any journal posts or reverses in that fiscal year, so you never see stale numbers. If in doubt, force-refresh with Ctrl+Shift+R.

Dimensional reports

Beyond the primary statements, you can run:

  • Budget vs Actual Comparison (IPSAS 24) — three-column variance report
  • Programme Performance Report — execution by NCoA Programme segment
  • Functional Classification (COFOG) — execution by Function
  • Geographic Distribution — execution by LGA / Zone
  • Revenue Performance — actuals vs revenue budget by Revenue Head

Walkthrough 2 — Review Override Audit

Tenant settings

The settings pages are only accessible to Tenant Admin users. They configure the behaviour of the entire tenant and the values every other module reads from.

Fiscal Year management

  1. Open Settings → Fiscal Year Management.
  2. Click New Fiscal Year.
  3. Set Year (e.g. 2027), Start Date (typically 1 Jan), End Date (31 Dec), and Status (usually Planning then advance to Active on Jan 1).
  4. The system auto-creates 12 monthly Fiscal Periods. You can re-scope them (e.g. to quarters) before any transactions are posted.

Accounting Settings

Configure default GL accounts used by automated postings (AP, AR, VAT, WHT, Pension, NHF, Cash, Accumulated Fund, etc.). Wrong defaults are the #1 cause of "journal failed to post" errors on downstream modules.

Tax setup

PAYE bands, VAT rate, WHT rates per nature-of-payment. Review before the start of each fiscal year.

Bank Accounts

Register every physical bank / cash / petty-cash account here first. Reference them from GL, Treasury and Payroll.

Brand Identity

Upload your State's logo, set the primary & accent colours, and the sidebar + emails + exported PDFs will pick them up automatically.

User management

The Tenant Admin role invites users, assigns roles, and can suspend/reset credentials.

Walkthrough — Invite a new user

  1. Open User Management.
  2. Click Invite User.
  3. Enter their Name, Email, and the Role (Budget Officer, Account Officer, etc.).
  4. Optionally assign them to specific MDAs / Departments to scope what they can see.
  5. Click Send Invite. An email goes out with the tenant URL and a temporary password.

Troubleshooting & FAQ

I can't post a journal — "period is CLOSED"
Your Accountant-General closed that period. Either re-date the journal to the current open period, or request a dual-control override (use sparingly; see Module 8).
My approval doesn't advance the document
(1) Check the approval matrix — there may be a level above yours required for this amount tier. (2) Check you aren't the initiator of the document; SOD blocks self-approval.
Dashboard numbers look stale
Hard refresh with Ctrl+Shift+R. If still stale, open Data Quality — any red-status item blocks accurate aggregates. Tenant Admin can trigger resync_appropriation_totals from the admin console.
Journal won't balance even though debits = credits
Check for rounding — each line amount must have exactly 2 decimal places. The grid sometimes displays a rounded value but stores a longer decimal. Re-enter suspicious lines.
The IPSAS report looks different from what I expected
Most "wrong" reports are caused by mis-coded journals — an expense posted to a revenue account, or the wrong Function. Open Data Quality → Missing NCoA codes first; 80% of the time the error is there.
My bulk import said "tenant not found"
The CSV was uploaded to the wrong tenant URL. Confirm you're on the correct State subdomain before importing.
PO approval says "budget exceeded" but there's budget left
The appropriation likely has an open Supplementary in the pipeline that hasn't yet been ENACTED. Check the Appropriation detail → Pending amendments tab.
FIRS rejected my XML
Open the schedule again, click Validate XML. The XSD-validation errors show the exact row + field. Usually it's a missing TIN on one invoice.
Notification bell won't update
The bell polls every 30 seconds. If it's been longer than 2 minutes, sign out and back in — your session token may have expired.
Where is my data stored?
In a dedicated PostgreSQL schema for your State, hosted in a Nigeria availability zone. Cross-tenant access is impossible at the database layer.

Glossary

AGAccountant-General of the State. The apex finance officer who signs IPSAS statements.
AIEAuthority to Incur Expenditure — a warrant instrument authorising an MDA to draw against an appropriation.
AppropriationThe legal spending authority created by the House of Assembly via the annual Appropriation Act.
CommitmentAn encumbrance created when a PO is approved, reducing the available balance on the parent appropriation.
COFOGUN Classification of Functions of Government — the 10 top-level function codes used on the NCoA Functional segment.
CRFConsolidated Revenue Fund — the main operating fund of a State.
FAACFederation Account Allocation Committee — the monthly distribution of federal revenue to States and LGAs.
FIRSFederal Inland Revenue Service — the federal tax authority.
GRNGoods Received Note — the document confirming receipt of goods against a PO.
IFMISIntegrated Financial Management Information System — the umbrella term for systems like Quot PSE.
IGRInternally Generated Revenue — non-federal State revenue (PAYE, fees, levies).
IPSASInternational Public Sector Accounting Standards.
MDAMinistry, Department, or Agency — any administrative unit of the State.
NCoANational Chart of Accounts — the 6-segment federal classification schema.
PENCOMNational Pension Commission.
PFAPension Fund Administrator — the entity managing an employee's Retirement Savings Account.
PFM ActPublic Finance Management Act — the umbrella law governing public-sector finance.
PVPayment Voucher — authorises disbursement of cash after the invoice is verified.
SODSegregation of Duties — the control rule that the user creating a document cannot also approve it.
SupplementaryA budget amendment that adds money to an existing appropriation (requires House of Assembly approval).
TSATreasury Single Account — consolidating all State cash accounts into a single view.
VirementA budget amendment that moves money between appropriations (zero-sum within the total budget).
WarrantThe AG's authorisation for a specific cash draw against an active appropriation.
WHTWithholding Tax — tax deducted at source on contracts, consultancy, rent, etc.
